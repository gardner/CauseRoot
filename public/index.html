Enabling You to Build a Better World

CauseRoot is a platform that enables you to submit the critical issues you are passionate about, in order to–as a community–discover the best solution and get results.

We help the community by launching technical challenges for creative solutions to address issues of public interest. Users can submit their own technical challenges, as well as promote and engage other submission topics that resonate with them. For challenges with high public sentiment, CauseRoot crowdfunds their prize purses to drive attention to problem areas not otherwise economically incentivized. We then host the funded competitions and award prizes for the best solutions. All submitted entries get published, so the community can collaborate and build upon the best attributes of each innovative solution. By participating, you help make the world a better place.

In a world of increasing complexity, we need to be smarter about how we address society's most pressing issues. Our collaborative platform enables you to voice the problems you want solutions for, but it also helps you to understand the most important issues that concern the majority of the community. As we aggregate the information from our solutions for positive change, the community benefits not only from the winning solutions, but from discovery of original ideas across all of the submissions. As a whole, users benefit from the wisdom of the crowd because it focuses our efforts on addressing the right problems with an aggregate of the best approaches.

At CauseRoot, our mission focuses on creating incentive where open market solutions have not yet been demonstrated as lucrative. Crowdfunding actions to address discrete issues of public concern allows these tasks to become actionable by talented individuals. Our platform model directs the motivations of the crowd to enable individuals to act on them. By funding the effort or by submitting solutions, you now have multiple ways to directly help fix problems that are most important to you.

Sound good? You'd be surprised what a couple of ex-Rocket Scientists come up with when they're motivated to see a change in the world. Join us!
